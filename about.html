About PEAK

Empowering refugees in Kent to access higher education and build brighter futures through personalized guidance and support.

Our Mission & Vision

PEAK was founded with a clear purpose: to break down barriers to higher education for refugees in Kent. We believe that education is a powerful tool for integration, empowerment, and rebuilding lives.

Our Mission

To provide personalized guidance, resources, and support to refugees in Kent who aspire to access higher education, helping them navigate the complex educational landscape and overcome barriers to academic success.

We aim to create clear pathways to universities, connect refugees with appropriate resources, and advocate for inclusive educational policies that recognize the unique challenges faced by displaced individuals.

Our Vision

A Kent where every refugee with the desire to pursue higher education has the information, support, and opportunities needed to achieve their academic goals and fulfill their potential.

We envision a future where educational institutions fully embrace the talents, perspectives, and contributions of refugee students, creating diverse and inclusive learning environments that benefit all.

Meet the Founder

Osama Sharkia

Founder & Director

As the founder of PEAK, I am deeply committed to creating educational opportunities for refugees in Kent. With firsthand understanding of the challenges faced by those seeking to rebuild their lives through education, I established PEAK to provide the guidance I wish had been available to me.

My background in education and personal experience navigating the UK higher education system as a refugee has shaped PEAK's approach to providing practical, empathetic support. I believe that education is not just about academic achievement, but about restoring dignity, purpose, and hope.

Through PEAK, I aim to create a supportive community that empowers refugees to overcome barriers and access the transformative power of higher education.

Anticipated Impact

As we prepare to launch PEAK, we're excited about the positive change we aim to create in the lives of refugees seeking higher education in Kent. Here's the impact we anticipate making:

Supporting 100+ Refugees

We aim to provide personalized guidance to over 100 refugees in our first year, helping them navigate pathways to higher education in Kent.

University Placements

We anticipate helping at least 50 refugees secure places at universities in Kent, with appropriate financial support and accommodation.

Community Building

We plan to create a supportive community of current and aspiring refugee students who can share experiences and provide peer support.

Educational Resources

We will develop comprehensive, accessible resources tailored to the specific needs of refugees seeking higher education in Kent.

Personalized Consultations

We expect to conduct over 200 one-to-one consultations, providing individualized guidance on education pathways and options.

Institutional Partnerships

We aim to establish strong partnerships with all universities in Kent to create more inclusive policies and dedicated support for refugee students.

Our Journey

PEAK is just beginning its journey to transform higher education access for refugees in Kent. Here's our roadmap for the future:

2025

PEAK Launch

Official launch of the PEAK website and services, providing online resources and initial consultations for refugees seeking higher education in Kent.

2025-2026

Building Foundations

Establishing partnerships with universities, refugee organizations, and community groups. Developing comprehensive resources and support systems.

2026-2027

Expanding Services

Introducing mentorship programs, regular workshops, and expanding our team to reach more refugees across Kent.

2027-2028

Creating Community

Developing a strong network of refugee students and graduates who can provide peer support and inspiration to newcomers.

Beyond

Systemic Change

Working with institutions to implement lasting policy changes that make higher education more accessible for all refugees in the UK.
